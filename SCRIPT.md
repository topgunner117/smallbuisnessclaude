# Claude for Small Business — Presenter Script (short version)

Slides used: 1, 05, 06, then the six demos. Advance quickly through everything in between.

## Setup
- Open index.html, press F11 for fullscreen
- Right arrow or space for next slide, left arrow to go back
- Demo slides: the prompt types itself when you arrive. A coral send button appears when it finishes. The demo waits until you click it or press Enter.
- If a demo looks stuck: arrow back, then forward. It resets and replays.

### SLIDE 1 · Title

- This talk is about what Claude actually does for a small business, using the tools you already run.
- The rule for everything in this talk: it drafts, you approve, nothing sends itself.

ADVANCE (skip past slides 02 to 04)

### SLIDE 05 · Growth: HubSpot and Canva

- HubSpot: scores inbound leads by fit and urgency. Logs calls, notes, and deal updates so the CRM stays current without anyone doing data entry.
- Canva: turns a brief into on-brand social posts, captions, and a posting calendar, in your colors and fonts.

ADVANCE

### SLIDE 06 · Admin: DocuSign, Google, Microsoft

- DocuSign: reads contracts, flags non-standard terms in plain English, preps a redline. A first pass, not a lawyer.
- Google Workspace and Microsoft 365: same abilities on either side. Draft and queue emails, block calendar time, work from your docs, spreadsheets, and files.

## DEMOS

Rhythm each time: state the problem as you arrive, the prompt types itself (about 5 seconds), coral button pulses, CLICK SEND, narrate the results.

### SLIDE 17 · Demo 1: Chase the money

THE PROBLEM (say as you arrive):
- Customers pay late. Chasing them means checking QuickBooks by hand and writing awkward reminder emails one at a time.
- Most owners let it slide, so the money sits uncollected for weeks.

While it types:
- The prompt is plain English. Find every invoice more than two weeks overdue, draft a reminder to each customer.

CLICK SEND

Results:
- QuickBooks search runs first: four invoices found.
- Gmail creates four drafts.
- $5,425 outstanding, the worst invoice is 31 days late.
- Banner: nothing has been sent. Four drafts waiting for approval.

ADVANCE

### SLIDE 18 · Demo 2: Follow up the leads

THE PROBLEM:
- New leads go cold because nobody follows up in time. The fastest responder usually wins the job.
- Nobody has time to scroll the CRM and figure out who is worth calling first.

While it types:
- Find this week's leads that nobody contacted, rank them, draft follow-ups.

CLICK SEND

Results:
- Twelve leads came in, five were never contacted.
- Ranked by deal size. Top lead is worth about $12,000 a year and asked for a quote by Friday.
- Five drafts waiting, nothing sends without approval.

ADVANCE

### SLIDE 19 · Demo 3: Make the marketing

THE PROBLEM:
- Running a promo takes a designer, an email tool, and a free afternoon. So most promos never happen.

While it types:
- A spring promo, 15% off through April. Flyer plus announcement email.

CLICK SEND

Results (three tool cards this time):
- Canva generates the flyer.
- HubSpot pulls the customer list: 86 contacts.
- Gmail drafts the announcement with the flyer attached.
- The flyer comes in two sizes, email and Instagram, in the brand colors.

ADVANCE

### SLIDE 20 · Demo 4: Chase the signatures

THE PROBLEM:
- Work cannot start until the contract is signed, but contracts sit unsigned for days.
- Following up feels awkward, so nobody does it, and deals stall.

While it types:
- Find contracts sitting unsigned in DocuSign for more than five days.

CLICK SEND

Results:
- Three contracts stuck. One expires in two days, shown with the red chip.
- Reminders staged, matched to how long each has waited.
- You hit send, not Claude.

ADVANCE

### SLIDE 21 · Demo 5: Balance the books

THE PROBLEM:
- Payments come in through PayPal but invoices live in QuickBooks. Matching them by hand takes an hour.
- Partial payments and jobs that never got invoiced slip through unnoticed. That is real money lost.

While it types:
- Match last week's PayPal payments against open QuickBooks invoices.

CLICK SEND

Results:
- 23 payments, 21 matched automatically.
- Flag 1: Bluebird Cafe (from demo 1) paid $430 on an $860 invoice. Partial payment.
- Flag 2: a $175 payment with no matching invoice. A job that was never billed. Found money.
- Green banner: about 45 minutes of bookkeeping done automatically.

ADVANCE

### SLIDE 22 · Demo 6: Put it on a schedule

THE PROBLEM:
- Owners find out about cash problems and overdue invoices too late.
- Pulling a weekly overview means opening three tools on a Monday morning, so it never happens.

While it types:
- Every demo so far was asked for manually. This one is asked once and runs forever.

CLICK SEND

Results:
- One line, "every Monday at 8am," creates a standing job.
- The preview shows cash in, cash out, net for the week, new leads, overdue invoices.
- It lands in the inbox every Monday without anyone asking again. That is the difference between a chatbot and a coworker.
